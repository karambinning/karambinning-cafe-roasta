01 · About

A working caff, not a concept café.

Aya's family have been quietly feeding King's Lynn from this spot on Tower Street for years. The model is honest and unfashionable: home-cooked traditional English dishes prepared in the back, brought out hot, laid on the counter, and ladled onto your plate carvery-style by whoever's serving that morning. You pay by what you take.

It's the kind of place a regulars' table forms in by half-past-eight. Builders before a job, market traders on a break, families in from out of town who've spotted the sign. The wall outside catches enough sun in the afternoon that the back tables feel more Mediterranean than Norfolk — though the menu, reliably, stays English.

No tablecloths. No reservations book. Walk in, queue at the counter, choose what looks best.

02 · What's on

Today, on the counter.

The spread changes through the week, but the spirit doesn't. Here's roughly what to expect when you push through the door.

  1. A1

    The Full English, all day.

    Bacon, sausage, eggs done how you like them, beans, mushrooms, tomato, hash brown, fried bread. Vegetarian version on request — the meat-free sausages are good.

    Until close · sit-in or take-away
  2. A2

    The Buffet from the counter.

    Six or seven hot dishes laid out carvery-style — roast beef one day, gammon another, a vegetable curry, a pasta bake, plenty of veg. Pile your plate, pay by what you take.

    Mid-morning onwards
  3. A3

    Sunday Roast.

    Beef, pork or turkey with Yorkshires, three or four veg, roast potatoes, gravy from the joints. Comes out from late morning until it runs out — and it does run out.

    Sundays · while it lasts
  4. A4

    Jackets, sandwiches & soup.

    Baked potatoes with cheese, beans, tuna, chilli. Sandwiches and toasties to order. Home-made soup of the day with crusty bread.

    Lunchtime · take-away too
  5. A5

    Cakes & bakes, made here.

    Victoria sponge, fruit cake, lemon drizzle, the occasional tray-bake. Baked in the back. If you see one, it won't be there long.

    All day · ask what's fresh
  6. A6

    Tea, coffee & the rest.

    Builder's brew by the mug, cafetière coffee, cans of fizz, juice for the kids. Bottomless refills aren't a thing, but the kettle's never far away.

    From 8am
